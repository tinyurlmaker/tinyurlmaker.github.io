Experiment details

Each phishing email used in this study was uniquely generated for the recipient. A Large Language Model (LLM) retrieved publicly available information from social media platforms (Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, X) and incorporated these elements into the message to increase plausibility.

No private profiles, restricted sections, or direct messages were accessed. The system did not store participants' personal data beyond the automated generation of the message. The only information recorded during the experiment was whether the recipient clicked on the link contained in the email.

Debrief

This was a simulated phishing attempt conducted exclusively for research purposes at Politecnico di Milano, Department of Electronics, Information and Bioengineering (DEIB), NECSTLab. The study aims to understand how personalized phishing campaigns, powered by modern AI systems, may affect user behavior.

You are not at risk: no credentials or sensitive data were collected, and all information used to generate the phishing email was deleted immediately after the message was sent. If you request a personalized report, a new search will be carried out to reproduce the process, and the results will be sent to your email. Following delivery, this data will also be deleted from the system.

The intent of this study is to improve awareness and strengthen defenses against increasingly sophisticated phishing techniques.
